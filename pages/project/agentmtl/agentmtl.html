Agent.run!(Montréal)

Unveiling the Rental Crisis
Agent-Based Modeling is a powerful tool to unravel the complexities within systems and unveil solutions for otherwise elusive problems. The housing crisis engulfing Montreal is a poignant example.

agent.run!(Montréal) immerses the viewer in an hypothetical simulation of a scaled-down city. Drawing from authentic statistical data, this simulation tries to replicates the intricate dance between tenants and landlords within the fictional urban landscape.

Navigate through clusters on the map, each representing a distinct neighbourhood. The intensity of brightness reflects the soaring rents and how affordability varies across this virtual city. Each dot signifies a tenant in an apartment, with red dots marking those residing above the affordability threshold.

This simulated metropolis transforms into a realm of diminishing accessibility, compelling an increasing number of its inhabitants that are pushed under the poverty line or are forced to leave the city.
